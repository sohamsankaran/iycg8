The G8

The Group of Eight (at IYC 2012)
The most prestigious committee at IYC

For hundreds of years, the countries that comprise the Group of Eight have utterly dominated the world, socially, politically, and economically, 14% of the human population ruling over the rest. But the once undisputed masters of the universe are in trouble. Their economies lie in shambles, overtaken by those they once called barbarians, their armies, routinely defeated by hordes of militants who will stop at nothing to see them vanquished forever. Once called the cabinet of the world, the G8 may now be fighting for its very survival, just as they are needed most. As lumbering giants turn underdogs, allegiances change at the drop of a hat, and the world spins deeper and deeper into the abyss of anarchy, the G8 alone has the power to restore balance before time runs out...

Structure: Crisis Committee

The G8 this year is structured as an HMUN-style crisis committee. What does that mean, you ask? Well, it means that delegates are thrust, from the very beginning, into an emergency situation they must resolve, and have at their disposal, theoretically at least, the might of the country they represent. They can issue requests to their respective governments to carry out almost any action (short of self-destruction), and then watch as the consequences of those actions play out in committee. The final product of this type of committee is not a MUN Resolution, but rather actual resolution of a crisis situation.

You aren't limited to purely theoretical discussions of your proposals; you can put them into action straight away, at least in lands you or your allies control. But that also means that things could go awry very quickly. The spies you sent out to Japan could get caught and confess. Your secret weapon could get sabotaged. Your army chief could be bought of by the enemies. These are perilous times.

tl;dr The G8 is your chance to participate in a unique, realtime, no holds barred simulation of an emergency situation that you can directly interact with. This is global diplomacy at its visceral best.

Put down your working papers and draft resolutions and come resolve a real crisis.

Prelude

In August 2012, a vial of the immeasurably deadly H5N1 virus, a sibling of the H1N1 virus, disappears from a research lab in Siberia. Its creators later admit that this version of the virus was mutated so as to allow airborne transmissions between mammals.

A single millilitre of the culture could theoretically kill off an entire country....

Just minutes after the Russian federation calls for a special summit of the G8, an unidentified male walks into Interpol offices in Seoul, South Korea.....

He carries with him a sample of the virus....

Topics for discussion

1. The mutated H5N1 virus and potent genetically modified virii or bacteria that are harmful to mankind in general.

2. The Korean border and the actions of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (i.e. North Korea).

3. The situation in Syria.

Note that as the crises unfold, the situation may get much more complicated, and other factors or factions may come into play. On an unrelated note, I hear Georgia's nice this time of the year.
